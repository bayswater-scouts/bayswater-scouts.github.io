On our way to another adventure!

  • 2nd/3rd Bayswater Scout Group is an active and dynamic scout group, with members across five sections aged between 6 and 25. One of two Scout Groups in Bayswater, we have been a part of the local community for over fifty years.

    Our Group prides itself in encouraging the out in Scouting, with many activities taking place beyond our Scout Hall — located right next to Bayswater Railway Station.

    As Scouts, our mission is to contribute to the education of young people, through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, to help build a better world where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play a constructive role in society.

Scouting around the World

  • The 2nd/3rd Bayswater Scout Group not only welcomes you into our family but also into the worldwide Scouting Movement.

    Our Group, together with every other Group in the municipality, is part of the City of Knox District. Perspective members are free to join any Group in their area, but it is best to join a Group as local as possible.

    Scouting is a worldwide movement with over 40 million members in 180 countries. All Scouts, regardless of colour, race or religion, make the Scout Promise and share the ideals originally inspired by the founder of Scouting, Baden-Powell of Gilwell, over 100 years ago.

    Today, Scouting is the world's largest youth organisation, and your child has become a member of our Group, but that is not all, you, their parents or guardians, have joined our movement as well. Scouting is a cooperative community venture, sharing with family, school, and spiritual groups the task of building the character of the young - and you, the parents, are just as involved as your child.

Famous Scouts

  • Many famous people got their start through their time in the Scouts.

    Some former scouts you may have heard of include Rove McManus, David Parkin, Bert Newton, Catriona Noble, Sir Jack Brabham, Peter Garrett, Tim Jarvis, Sue-Anne Webster, Billy Conolly, David Attenborough, and Neil Armstrong.

    In fact, of the twelve astronauts who walked on the moon during NASA's Apollo missions between 1969 and 1972, only James Irwin of Apollo 15 was not a Scout growing up.
